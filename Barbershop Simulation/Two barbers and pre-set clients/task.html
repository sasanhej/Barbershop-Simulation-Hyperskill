Description

Your barbershop is open from 9 am, and the clients come at regular intervals of \(m\) minutes (the first client arrives precisely at 9 am); the first barber serves each visitor in s minutes, and the second one does it in \(t\) minutes. When a new client comes, they go to an unoccupied barber. If they are both free, the client goes to the barber who is free for a more extended period of time; if both barbers are unoccupied for the same period of time, the client goes to the first master. If both barbers are busy, clients queue in line. At 4 pm, the barbershop closes. All those who come on that day before the closing time are served; no exceptions.

Based on this information, compute the following quantities for every client:

  1. Arrival time: when did the client come to the barbershop?
  2. Waiting time: how long did they wait in line?
  3. Barber: the first one or the second one?

To determine the waiting time in the queue and the barber for each new client, it is suitable to calculate the values \(a_{1,i}\) and \(a_{2,i}\), where \(a_{1,i}\) is the number of minutes after 9 am when the first barber is available, assuming that only the first \(i\) clients will come on that day, and \(a_{2,i}\) is the same value for the second barber. For example, \(a_{1,1} = s\), \(a_{2,1} = 0\). When the \(i+1\)-th client arrives at the \(t_{i+1}\) time, based on \(t_{i+1}\), \(a_{1,i}\), and \(a_{2,i}\), we can determine which barber will be available first, and how long the client will have to wait in line. Then calculate \(a_{1,i+1}\) and \(a_{2,i+1}\) and process the \(i+2\)-th client.

Objectives

Write a program that takes three numbers \(m\), \(s\), and \(t\) as input separated by a space, computes the arrival time, the waiting time, and the barber for every client who came before the closing time. Ensure the program prints this data for each client on a separate line in the order they arrive.

Output format:

If you've had \(n\) clients today, the output should consist of \(n\) lines, where the \(i\)-th line refers to the \(i\)-th client and has the form of \(a\:w\:m\) where:

  • \(a\) is the arrival time of the \(i\)-th visitor in minutes after 9:00 am,
  • \(w\) is their waiting time in minutes,
  • \(m\) is the barber, 1 or 2.
